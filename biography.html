Biography

Youngseo Kim, born in 1992 in Seoul, South Korea, began his musical education at YeWon Art Middle School. In 2008, he moved to Germany to study at the Pre-College of the Robert Schumann Hochschule Düsseldorf with Prof. Ida Bieler. He completed his Master’s degree in Düsseldorf in 2018 with top honors. He then pursued a second Master’s degree in Contemporary Music Ensemble with Annette Bik and Prof. Jean-Bernard Matter at the University of Music and Performing Arts Vienna. Currently, he is enrolled in the Artist Diploma of Ensemble NEXT with Ensemble Intercontemporain at the Conservatoire National Supérieur de Musique et de Danse de Paris. Youngseo Kim has won numerous awards, including prizes at the Asian Music Competitions (U12), the 15th Korean Youth Competition, and the 30th Sun Hwa Arts School Competition. In 2007, he was awarded First Prize at the 18th Korean Youth Competition, a Gold Medal at the National Competition of the Guri Philharmonic Orchestra, and a Silver Medal at the National Competition of the Sejong Arts and Culture Association. During his time in Germany, Youngseo continued to achieve significant success. He won Second Prize at the RSH Pre-College J.U.S.T. Competition (2010), Second Prize in the Solo Violin category at the national “Jugend Musiziert” competition (2010), and Third Prize in the Chamber Music category at the same competition (2011). As a soloist, he has performed with the Guri Philharmonic Orchestra and the NWD Philharmonic. Additionally, he played at the 40th Liesborner Chamber Music Festival, had multiple performances at the Arnold Schönberg Center in Vienna, and gave concerts at the Musikverein Wien with the Argo Kollektiv, at the Wiener Konzerthaus, at the Salzburg Festival with Klangforum Wien, at the Philharmonie de Paris and at the Festival Présences in Paris. Youngseo has been awarded scholarships for the Eastern Music Festival in Greensboro (USA) and by the Yehudi Menuhin Live Music Now Association. He has participated in international masterclasses with Prof. Ida Bieler in Germany and Italy, as well as with Robert Davidovici, Anke Schittenhelm, and Kevin Lawrence. In 2023, he attended the International Darmstadt Summer Course for Contemporary Music. He also participated in a masterclass with the Arditti Quartet and attended the Freiburg Academy Week, where he worked with Ensemble Recherche. Additionally, Youngseo was a member of the German National Youth Orchestra (Bundesjugendorchester) and completed an internship with the Bochum Symphony Orchestra in 2016/17. He is a founding member of the Ensemble Argo Kollektiv, Tacet Quartet and the Ensemble Kreis. With extensive teaching experience, he was involved in the Vivaldi Program at the Robert Schumann Hochschule Düsseldorf, where he taught underprivileged children to play the violin. He also worked as a teacher at the Ida Bieler Music Academy in Cologne.Youngseo Kim is a violinist and artistic researcher whose work interrogates the intersection of musical performance, epistemology, and socio-critical structures. They are currently pursuing an Artistic Research PhD at the University of Music and Performing Arts Vienna (mdw), with doctoral research focusing on the non-verbal social criticism embedded within the re-contextualization of Fluxus performance protocols.

Kim’s artistic praxis spans both contemporary music (Neue Musik) and historical performance practice. They have performed extensively on major international stages, including the Salzburg Festival, collaborating with leading avant-garde ensembles such as Klangforum Wien. Currently based in Paris, Kim actively bridges rigorous academic inquiry with critical stage practice.

Youngseo Kim est violoniste et chercheur en art (Artistic Research), dont les travaux explorent l'intersection entre la performance musicale, l'épistémologie et les structures de la critique sociale. Il poursuit actuellement un doctorat en recherche artistique à l'Université de musique et des arts du spectacle de Vienne (mdw). Sa recherche doctorale porte sur la critique sociale non-verbale à travers la recontextualisation des protocoles de performance Fluxus.

Sa pratique artistique embrasse à la fois la musique contemporaine (Neue Musik) et la musique ancienne (Historical Performance Practice). Il s'est produit sur de grandes scènes internationales, notamment au Festival de Salzbourg, en collaborant avec des ensembles de premier plan tels que Klangforum Wien. Actuellement basé à Paris, Kim relie de manière organique l'investigation académique et la performance scénique critique.

바이올리니스트이자 예술적 연구(Artistic Research)학자인 김영서는 음악적 수행과 사회문화적 맥락의 상호작용을 깊이 있게 탐구해 왔다. 오스트리아 비엔나 국립음악예술대학교(mdw) 예술적 연구(Artistic Research) 박사과정에서 플럭서스(Fluxus) 퍼포먼스 구조의 재맥락화를 통한 비언어적 사회비판 연구를 진행 중이다.

현대 음악(Neue Musik)의 수행적 방법론과 중세·르네상스·바록에 이르는 역사적 연주 관습(Historical Performance Practice) 모두에 깊은 관심을 두고 있으며, 클랑포룸 빈(Klangforum Wien) 등 세계적인 현대음악 앙상블과의 협업을 통해 잘츠부르크 페스티벌을 비롯한 주요 국제 무대에서 연주자로 활동해 왔다. 현재 프랑스 파리를 기반으로 연주 수행과 학술 연구를 유기적으로 연결하는 독자적인 예술적 영토를 구축하고 있다.
